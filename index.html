BEST MAN SPEECH
~700 words. ~5 min. After sunset photos ~7:30 PM Fri.
I'm Mac. Timmy's best man. We go back to the rink. We both played youth hockey at SIR. I was a couple years older, worked there part time. Tim had this long hair he'd swing out of his eyes every time he came through the lobby door. That's the first thing I ever knew about him. We just wanted to play hockey. All the time. After hours, weeknights, didn't matter. That started when we were teenagers and honestly never stopped. We ended up in beer league together and most people assumed he was my little brother. I always took that as a compliment. He used the same line in his best man speech at my wedding. So it stuck with both of us. He did knock two of my teeth out though. Pickup skate. I made a dumb play trying to stop him. Timmy dangles. Two teeth, bit my tongue on the way down. Ruined the vibe of the whole skate. Honestly I was over it pretty quick. Thankful it was Tim. Bound to happen sometime. Sorry Tim. If you've been holding on to that, you can let it go tonight. You can learn a lot about someone by the way they play hockey. Whether they force things or let the game come to them. Whether they lift the guys around them or just try to stand out. Tim plays both sides of the puck. He's an offensive threat who plays defense like he's protecting something that matters. Because he's so reliable, everyone around him plays with more confidence. Endless energy. Positive attitude. Sportsmanship award. That's not just hockey. That's who he is everywhere. He could walk into a room and command correct anyone in it. But that's not how he works. He wants the people around him to feel capable and included. He's apologetic when he's right. He's always been up for a thought experiment, a real conversation about people or reality or what any of it means. That's Tim. And he's never once needed to be sorry for any of it. I spent a lot of years trying to get this guy to take a risk. Go somewhere without knowing every detail ahead of time. Because only when you don't know what's going to happen can you experience the thrill of it happening. I had moderate success. Then there's Nikki. Nikki did what I and a lot of people in this room had been trying to do for a long time. She figured him out. I started noticing things. Tim still showed up to every skate. Never bailed. Never left early. But he stopped hanging around after. Used to be the guy who'd close the night down. Then it was 45 minutes after the game. Two beers at the bar and food to go. He was regimented about it. Not erratic. He had a reason to get home and he was protecting it before he even had the words for why. We lived through our twenties and not a lot changed. I got engaged. COVID hit. Nikki was moving to California for her teaching career and a pandemic wasn't going to change her mind. Then Timmy told me, less than ten days before he left, that he was going too. Seat belt on. Nervous excitement. A small sadness but open to the next chapter and whatever it could be. He quit his job. Followed the girl he loved across the country. Figured it out when he got there. Only when you don't know what's going to happen can you experience the thrill of it happening. He took his own advice. Now they're heading back to Western New York so Tim can finish his doctorate. The kid with the long hair at SIR has tattoos and says hella. Not surprised. Not even a little. Tim was my best man. COVID wedding. Fifty people. Backyard. He stood next to me when it counted. When he asked me to stand here, I understood what that meant. Nikki, take good care of him. He's dependable. He's positive. He cares about everyone and just wants things to be better. He won't say it so I'll say it for him. He's one of the great ones. Timmy, you're a great human I loved playing hockey with, who I would always pick for my team. I'm honored you picked me for yours. Raise a glass. To Timmy and Nikki. You chased the sun to the Pacific and built something real. May you carry that warmth wherever you go next. Even back to Western New York in January. Cheers.
